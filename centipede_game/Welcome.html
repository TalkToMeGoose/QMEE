How the game works:

You will be paired with another participant for the entirety of the game. Each round you will be randomly assigned either the red or blue player.
You will play {{ C.NUM_ROUNDS }} rounds of a game, either taking a given set of payoffs or passing the decision to the other player.

Example of a round:


On the red player's turn, they must decide whether to 'take' or 'pass'.
If the red player takes at the first decision point, the round ends and the payoffs 💰5 are awarded for red and 💰3 for blue.
If the red player passes, it moves to the blue player's decision whether to 'take' or 'pass'.

Important things to note:

✍ If both players pass at every decision point, the final payoffs shown at the far right are awarded shown as (320,192).
✍ The round ends and payoffs are awarded whenever a player takes or when players pass on all nodes.
✍ Before the start of each round, both players will see a preview of the round's payoffs.
✍ After completing all rounds, your total payoff across rounds will be shown. The top three scoring players will be rewarded by the experimenters.

If you have any questions or need help during the experiment, please ask an examiner. Please look only at your own device during this experiment.
